## The Final Three — What Separates Good From Legendary

### 🔥 1. The Features Section: Stop Treating All Children Equally

*"This section is the page's one remaining weakness. Six cards, all identical structure, all equally weighted. Your eye has no anchor. In advertising, we call this 'the committee layout' — everything is important, so nothing is."*

**The Fix:** Make **Multimodal Analysis** the hero feature. Give it a large visual (a side-by-side: raw video frame vs. PremiumBrain's extracted data points). Then list the other 5 as compact pills beneath it. This transforms the section from "feature dumping" to "proof of magic."

**Effort:** Medium | **10x?:** Yes — this is the single weakest section

---

### 🔥 2. Problem Section: Animate the Pain

*"Your three statistics (19.8%, 42%, €15,400) are powerful. But they sit there. Dead. Static. A statistic that APPEARS from zero stays in the mind 3× longer than one that was already there when you arrived."*

**The Fix:** Add a count-up animation. When the user scrolls the Problem section into view, the numbers count from 0 to their value. `19.8%` should tick up digit by digit. `€15,400` should climb like a financial counter. Use Intersection Observer.

**Effort:** Small | **10x?:** Borderline, but it transforms the strongest copywriting section into an experiential one

---

### 🔥 3. Final CTA: Add a Micro-Commitment

*"'Ready to Give Your Company a Brain?' is a strong question. But after an entire page of evidence, the close feels generic. Every SaaS page says 'Book a Demo.' Yours needs to say MORE."*

**The Fix:** Add a specific, time-bounded commitment beneath the CTA button: 
- Change "Free demo · No commitment · Your data, your language" → **"15 minutes. Your data. We'll show you answers your team can't find today."**

**Effort:** Tiny | **10x?:** It sharpens the close from generic to irresistible
